The Reciprocity Theorem

The Conjugated Form

The reciprocity theorem relates the line integral of a field to the surface integral of its divergence. The field under consideration here is a combination of the electric E and the magnetic field H, given by $$ \mathbf{F}_c = \bf{E} \times \bar{\bf{H}} + \bar{\bf{E}} \times \bf{H} \ , $$

where the barred quantities denote the Fourier space representation of the fields. Applying the divergence theorem, we find

$$ \int_{A} \nabla \cdot \textbf{F}_c \ dA = \frac{\partial}{\partial z} \int_A \textbf{F}_c \cdot \hat{z} \ dA + \int \textbf{F}_c \cdot \hat{n} \ dl \ . $$

The second term on the right hand side of the above equation represents a line integral along the boundary of A. In the limit of infinite cross section, the line integral drops to zero as physical fields are assumed to be zero at infinity. Thus, we are left with the reciprocity theorem, $$ \frac{\partial}{\partial z} \int_A \textbf{F}_c \cdot \hat{z} \ dA = \int_A \nabla \cdot \textbf{F}_c \ dA \ . $$
